Click map analysis

Where users physically click or tap

Click activity is strongest around areas that already look actionable: the header, hero buttons, main CTAs, bike and product cards, FAQ sections, and contact areas. Users are not passive, they are interacting and trying to move forward.

Observed behaviour
  • Top navigation and hero area receive meaningful interaction.
  • Product areas and bike cards receive interaction, a sign visitors want to compare models.
  • Some clicks land on images and text, so users may expect these to be clickable.
  • Lower-page CTAs and contact areas get less opportunity because fewer users scroll that far.
Recommendations
  • Make every product image and card clickable, routing to the right product or enquiry path.
  • Use clearer CTA labels: Book a Test Ride, Call Whangarei Team, View This Bike, Ask About This Model.
  • Repeat the main CTA after the local Whangarei section and around product cards.
  • Track phone clicks, booking clicks, product card clicks, FAQ opens, and form submissions.

Click insight. Users are clicking where they expect action. The opportunity is to make those paths clearer and easier to complete.

Move map analysis

Where users move their pointer

A helpful signal for attention and scanning, especially on desktop. Attention is strongest in the upper half, then becomes more scattered lower down, which matches the scroll data.

Observed behaviour
  • Strong attention around the navigation, hero content, and CTA button area.
  • The local person/bike section draws meaningful attention and helps build trust.
  • Product cards receive attention, indicating interest in specific models.
  • Attention becomes less concentrated lower down, matching the scroll drop-off.
Recommendations
  • Use the upper half to answer the key questions: what is offered, why trust MeloYelo Whangarei, and what to do next.
  • Place short proof points near the top: local support, test rides, servicing, reviews, and available models.
  • Use visual hierarchy so the main CTA stands out more than secondary options.
  • Keep lower sections useful, but do not place the only conversion path there.

Move insight. Visitor attention is strongest before the lower-page drop-off, so the page should sell and convert earlier.

Whangarei summary

The Whangarei page is generating interest, especially in the top section and upper-middle content. Users are engaging, but the lower sections are not seen by most visitors. The most important conversion issue is placement: the contact form is reached by only 13% of users, and the footer by only 9.9%. Simple client takeaway. Users are interested, but we need to give them a clear action before they stop scrolling.
